# Investigating the FLASH effect with Monte Carlo simulations

**Jan Schuemann**

*Department of Radiation Oncology, Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School*

---

The FLASH effect, the observed dramatic sparing of healthy tissue at ultra-high dose rates (typically \>40 Gy/s) without compromising tumor control, has the potential to revolutionize radiation therapy. Yet over a decade since its (re-)discovery, the underlying mechanism remains actively debated. Leading hypotheses center around differences in chemical reactions due to the short time scales involved in FLASH irradiations of a few milliseconds, including oxygen depletion and altered radical chemistry. However, experimental investigations at these sub-second processes face inherent limitations on temporal resolution. Track-structure Monte Carlo (MC) codes can fill this gap, offering unique capabilities to probe these early physical and chemical processes with unparalleled time resolution. In this presentation, I will introduce the TOPAS-nBio MC framework and demonstrate how we use these simulations to test competing hypotheses of the FLASH effect from inter-track reactions to oxygen dynamics. Identifying, or ruling out, potential mechanisms will be essential for clinical translation of FLASH tissue sparing.
