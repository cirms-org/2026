# Phytosanitary irradiation in international trade

**Maria Emilia Bustos Griffin** <br>
*JB TRINI Associates, Phytosanitary Solutions, 172 Roan Drive Garner NC 27529* <br>

---

International trade in fresh fruit and vegetables is vital for the economy and markets of many countries, but these products often require phytosanitary treatment to prevent the introduction of plant pests. Phytosanitary Irradiation (PI) has developed a leading role among current phytosanitary risk management strategies based on decades of research on the safety and efficacy of the technology. Experience with implementation of the technology in commercial applications has demonstrated the advantages of this treatment and convinced many different export sectors to adopt it.

PI is unique among phytosanitary measures because the target pest needs only be sterilized to eliminate the risk of introduction. This requires a change in the mindset of agriculture inspectors who are accustomed to traditional treatments that kill pests. Inspectors may struggle to understand and accept that pest mortality is not necessary for treatment to be effective.

The International Plant Protection Convention (IPPC) harmonized the phytosanitary treatment world in 2003 with the publication of the international standard No 18 “Guidelines for the use of irradiation as a phytosanitary measure”. This standard facilitated the development of national regulations such as those published by USDA-APHIS that allow the use of irradiation to treat numerous imported fruit and vegetables. Similar regulations were put in place between Australia and New Zealand.

Mexico is the largest user of PI. The first shipment of PI treated fruit was guavas to the United States in 2008. Since that date the volume of guavas and other fruits treated with PI from Mexico has steadily increased.

Today, more than 15 countries have established trade in PI treated fruit, but the volume of fresh irradiated commodities is still low compared to other treatments. Each country that has the possibility to take advantage of this technology needs private sector interest and investment and government cooperation to organize the regulatory and business plans that create frameworks for operation. These need to include outreach campaigns to educate investors and consumers.
