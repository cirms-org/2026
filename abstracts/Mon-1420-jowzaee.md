# The Effect of E-beam Irradiation Direction on Product Dose Uniformity and Dosimeter Response

**Ayeh Jowzaee** <br>
*STERIS Applied Sterilization Technologies, Libertyville, IL 60048*

---

In radiation processing, dose mapping is performed on products determine the location and magnitude of delivered dose to ensure product dose specifications are met. The dose distribution inside a product could be affected by orientation of the product with respect to the direction of the radiation beam. The profile of dose distribution throughout the product volume being dose mapped may be assessed to understand the directional effect of the radiation beam.

In this work, a Monte Carlo simulation model has been used to study the effect of E-beam direction processing on the dose profile (measured by DUR, or ratio of maximum to minimum dose within the volume being dose mapped) using a medical phantom developed at STERIS. The phantom was designed using plastic syringes and PVC perforated sheets in five layers placed into a rectangular cardboard box. The phantom box was irradiated on its side, top, and along the syringe axis. Dose mapping is performed using both B3 and alanine dosimeters. The results show that a different DUR is achieved depending on the selected E-beam irradiation direction of the phantom.
