# Using TLDs for In-vivo Radiotherapy Dose Verification

**Cliff Hammer**

- Department of Medical Physics, University of Wisconsin – Madison, Madison, WI 53705

---

Thermoluminescent Dosimetry (TLD) remains a gold-standard methodology in radiotherapy quality assurance, offering a reliable and well-established approach to independent dose verification. This presentation provides an in-depth overview of the University of Wisconsin TLD Therapy Dose Verification services, from in-vivo patient dose monitoring to linear accelerator output QA. A general overview of TLD theory and its clinical application will be presented, followed by a detailed description of the end-to-end service infrastructure, from material characterization through final dose analysis and reporting. The advantages, limitations, and best practices for achieving accurate TLD dosimetry will be discussed, with an emphasis on mitigating uncertainty. As part of the OSL Dosimetry Alternatives session, differences between TLD and OSLD verification services will be highlighted.
