# A new online computer program for neutron activation analysis

**Rui Fang, Zaijing Sun**

- Department of Health Physics and Diagnostic Sciences, University of Nevada, Las Vegas, Las Vegas, NV 89154

---

Neutron Activation Analysis (NAA) is one of the most sensitive techniques for quantitative multi-element analysis. However, its application often involves extensive and time-consuming data processing, particularly when dealing with complex nuclear spectra and mixed irradiation conditions. To improve efficiency and accessibility, we developed the Nuclear Activation Analysis System (NAAS), an online NAA calculation platform implemented using PhP, SQL and Python. NAAS provides two primary functions: (1) retrieval of radionuclide properties and nuclear reaction data relevant to NAA, and (2) automated calculation of elemental concentrations based on user-supplied spectral information and irradiation parameters. With minimal user input, the system performs automated spectral analysis, identifies peaks associated with feasible nuclear reactions, and computes target elemental concentrations along with their associated uncertainties. The platform is accessible through a web-based, user-friendly interface designed for rapid and reliable analysis. By streamlining data processing, NAAS significantly reduces labor requirements and minimizes potential human error. It is particularly valuable for users who wish to apply neutron activation analysis but may not be familiar with the underlying nuclear reactions or activation calculations.
