# Creating an ultra-thin parallel plate ionization chamber for Flash dosimetry

**Shannon Holmes, Brian Hooten**

- Standard Imaging Inc., Middleton, WI 53562

---

A significant challenge in the clinical implementation of Flash radiation therapy is the difficulty of accurately measuring doses from ultra-high dose per pulse (DPP) beamlines using traditional dosimeters. Existing commercial ionization chambers exhibit extreme saturation and recombination effects in ultra-high DPP irradiation. Despite the large correction factors that are required, ionization chambers are still used in these conditions in many centers. Ionization chambers serve clinically as the reference standard for dose measurements in medical applications, providing traceability to the national standards laboratories, and they are the only type of active detector that is calibrated at secondary standard dosimetry laboratories.

To provide a more accurate link between traditional dose to water calibration techniques and ultra-high DPP beams, we developed and commercialized a new parallel plate chamber with an ultra-thin air gap. Ion recombination modeling, manufacturing repeatability, construction material options, and real-world chamber characterization all contributed to the final design of this unique chamber.
